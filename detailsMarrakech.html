Marrakech, Morocco

Marrakech is a big city in Morocco, known as the "Red City" because of its red stone buildings. It is located near the Atlas Mountains. The old part of the city, called the medina, is famous for its narrow streets, colorful markets (souks), and beautiful old palaces. Jemaa el-Fnaa square is the busy center where you can see artists, musicians, and food vendors. Marrakech is also known for its lovely gardens like the Majorelle Garden, a peaceful place with many plants and flowers. In Marrakech, you can enjoy Moroccan food, local art, and take trips to the mountains or the desert. It’s a city full of color, history, and culture.

Marrakech’s buildings

Marrakech is known for its beautiful red and pink buildings. The walls and doors have nice decorations with patterns called arabesques, which look like leaves and flowers. Inside, many buildings have colorful tiles called zellige with shapes like stars and squares. There is also carved wood and plaster that make the ceilings and arches look very nice. Many houses in Marrakech are called riads. They have a calm courtyard in the middle with plants and fountains. These houses show the traditional style of Morocco.
